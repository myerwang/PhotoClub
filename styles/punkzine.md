Create a punk editorial collage poster in a handmade torn-paper zine style, combining vintage magazine cutouts, ransom-note typography, screen-print textures, and gritty protest-poster aesthetics. Use a multi-panel grid layout filled with black-and-white portrait photography, bold cutout headlines, and high-contrast blocks of burnt orange, muted red, teal blue, mustard yellow, black, and cream. Include visual elements such as: a screaming young woman in the center, a punk male portrait, tired or emotionally detached faces, a butterfly, a black bird, an eye, lightning bolts, a tornado icon, a barren tree, cracked dry earth, pollution / garbage imagery, a light bulb, checkerboard motifs, and anarchy-style symbols. The poster should feel like a mix of 1970s punk zine, underground political poster, DIY editorial collage, Dada-inspired cut-paper composition, and rebellious music-cover artwork. Add rough paper edges, visible paper fibers, misprinted ink, print grain, worn textures, imperfect alignment, and distressed vintage poster details. Theme and mood: rebellion, youth unrest, dream vs reality, fragile earth, mental chaos, no rules, social criticism, environmental anxiety. Composition: vertical 4:5 poster, dense layout, bold central figure, multiple separate collage blocks, dramatic visual rhythm, strong handmade typography, high emotional tension.
